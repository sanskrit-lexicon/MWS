  18th-century origin-of-language theory; his only lexicography-adjacent paper concerns

- **Applied:** entry removed with an inline provenance comment, D20 precedent. The
  genuine critical survey of the field, if one is wanted in §1, is verified:
  **Vogel, C. (1979). *Indian Lexicography*. Wiesbaden: Harrassowitz (A History of Indian
  Literature V.4), pp. 303–401, ISBN 3447020105** — BSOAS review of record quotes:
  "Claus Vogel: Indian lexicography. (A History of Indian Literature. Vol. v … Fasc. 4.)

  ([Cambridge Core](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/bulletin-of-the-school-of-oriental-and-african-studies/article/abs/claus-vogel-indian-lexicography-a-history-of-indian-literature-vol-v-scientific-and-technical-literature-fasc-4-viii-pp-303401-wiesbaden-otto-harrassowitz-1979-dm-48/54D8B3EC2F6B75B59259E963541A63D8));

## 3 · Wiegand 1989 pages + Wiegand 1995 — verdict: **CORRECTED / figure removed**

- HSK 5.1's Wiegand articles, from the [BBAW bibliography's](https://kollokationen.bbaw.de/bib/w.html)
  verbatim entries: *Aspekte der Makrostruktur … alphabetische Anordnungsformen und ihre
  Probleme*, *S. 371-409*; *Der Begriff der Mikrostruktur: Geschichte, Probleme,
  Perspektiven*, *S. 409-462*; *Arten von Mikrostrukturen im allgemeinen einsprachigen
  Wörterbuch*, *S. 462-501*.
- The paper's in-text "(1989: 416–425)" therefore pointed **inside the concept-history
  article**, while the reference list named the **Makrostruktur** article — and the
  microstructure **typology** actually lives in **Arten von Mikrostrukturen, 462–501**.
  **Applied:** list entry split into **1989a** (Makrostruktur, subtitle added, 371–409)
  and **1989b** (Arten, 462–501); A.1 repointed to 1989b.
- **Terminological finding:** "semi-integrated" is not 1989 vocabulary at all — the
  *semiintegrierte Mikrostrukturen* concept dates from **Wiegand 1996, *Das Konzept der
  semiintegrierten Mikrostrukturen*, Wörterbücher in der Diskussion II, Niemeyer, 1–82**;
  the 1989 typology opposes integrated/non-integrated with partial subtypes. **Applied
  (vetoable):** A.1 reworded to the 1989 terms with the 1996 refinement cited (new entry
  1996b; the Mediostrukturen entry becomes 1996a, its pages 11–43 **CONFIRMED** against
  the [De Gruyter chapter record](https://www.degruyter.com/document/doi/10.1515/9783110937916-003/html)).
- **"Wiegand 1995" (8–12 indicators in learner dictionaries):** a genuine on-topic
  Wiegand 1995 exists (*Lexikographische Texte in einsprachigen Lernerwörterbüchern*,
  Popp Festschrift, Iudicium, 463–499), but **no source confirms the 8–12 figure** in it
  or anywhere. **Applied (vetoable):** the figure and the cite dropped — "large by
  learner-dictionary standards" — the D20 standard: no number without a verified source.
- Wiegand 2002 **CORRECTED** to the [Lexikos record](https://lexikos.journals.ac.za/pub/article/view/772):
  full title *…: Criticism and Suggestions*, pages 239–255 confirmed from the galley's

## 5 · Atkins & Rundell 2008, four page cites — verdict: **1 CONFIRMED · 3 MISMATCH, corrected**

Verified against the full text (printed-page mapping spot-checked); TOC: Ch. 6 "Planning
the dictionary" 160–199 · Ch. 7 "Planning the entry" 200–259 · Ch. 8 "Word senses"
263–316 · Ch. 9 "The lexical unit" 317–381 · Ch. 10 "Building the monolingual entry"
385–464.

- **(2008: 168), compounds-as-run-ons — MISMATCH, and the claim inverted A&R.** P. 168
  is phrasal idioms. A&R's run-on is "the section of a dictionary entry which holds
  **infrequent derived forms** of the headword" (p. 236; also 397–398), and on compounds
  they say the opposite of what the paper claimed: "The current tendency however is to
  make compounds into headwords **when space permits**" (p. 181). **Applied:** B.1
  restated — run-ons for derived forms (236, 397–398), headword-status-when-space-permits
  for compounds (181); MW's *unconditional* every-compound-a-record policy remains the
  scalability contrast, now stated accurately.
- **(2008: 199), "median density 4–5" — MISMATCH: not in the book.** P. 199 is Ch. 6's
  exercise/recommended-reading page; the strings "density" and "median" occur **zero
  times in the entire book**. The 4–5 figure is the paper's own count under A&R's
  entry-component typology (§7.2, 202–246). **Applied:** both occurrences (§7(i), A.4)
  now attribute the figure to our own count and cite the typology (2008: 202–246).
- **(2008: 263), etymology-as-separate-field — MISMATCH.** P. 263 opens the word-senses
  chapter. The separate-field guidance is at **p. 117** ("all the … etymology fields can
  be assigned to specialists") and **p. 208** (§7.2.2.5, etymology as an entry
  component). **Applied:** B.3 repointed to 117, 208.
- **(2008: 386), register tracking — CONFIRMED.** P. 386 (§10.1) verbatim: the database
  records "an indication of any stylistic, regional, subject-field, or other features
  that require a linguistic label (§9.2.9)" and "sociolinguistic data is logged". Kept
  as-is; fuller guidance sits at 376 ff. and 403 if a stronger page is ever wanted.

## 6 · Wiegand & Smit (eds.) 2013 — verdict: **WRONG-EDITORS, corrected** (second-pass catch)

An independent second verification pass (same session series, H1077; four parallel web
agents re-auditing the whole reference list to the Major-5 standard) caught one defect the
first pass left in place: the reference "Wiegand, H. E. & Smit, M. (eds.) (2013).
*Dictionaries: An International Encyclopedia of Lexicography*. Supplementary Volume. De
Gruyter" has the **wrong editors**. The volume is HSK 5.4 and its editors are **Gouws,
Heid, Schweickard & Wiegand** — there is no editor named Smit (Maria Smit is a Wiegand
translator/co-author on other works). Evidence, three independent records quoting the
full editor list verbatim:

- [ZrP 2014 review](https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/zrp-2014-0114/html?lang=en): "Rufus Gouws / Ulrich Heid / Wolfgang Schweickard / Herbert Ernst Wiegand (edd.), Dictionaries. An International Encyclopedia of Lexicography. Supplementary volume … (HSK, 5.4), Berlin/Boston, De Gruyter Mouton, 2013, 1579 p."
- [Springer *Lexicography* review](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40607-015-0013-8): "Rufus H. Gouws, Ulrich Heid, Wolfgang Schweickard and Herbert Ernst Wiegand (eds.) … (HSK Vol. 5.4). Berlin: De Gruyter Mouton, 2013. XIII + 1,579 pp. ISBN 978-3-11-023812-9"
- [Project MUSE review](https://muse.jhu.edu/article/604749/pdf): "ed. by Rufus Gouws et al."

**Applied to PAPER.md (vetoable):** reference entry replaced with the Gouws/Heid/
Schweickard/Wiegand form (HSK 5.4, Berlin/Boston: De Gruyter Mouton); the Appendix A
framing cite "Wiegand & Smit 2013" → "Gouws et al. 2013".
