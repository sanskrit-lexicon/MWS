## Kill-gate verdict: STOP — these are human-vote packets

Each packet's "judgement" column is a genuine Sanskrit philological call, not
the execution of an upstream-decided rule:

- **Packet A** (`ib.` resolution, 50 items) — asks whether the resolver's
  document-order guess for an "ibidem" citation is the source MW actually
  meant. Deciding this requires reading MW's gloss in context and judging
  authorial intent; there is no independent oracle to check the resolver
  against.
- **Packet B** (band-3 `L.`→DCS, 167 items) — asks whether a DCS corpus
  sentence uses MW's headword **in MW's sense** (confirm) or is a
  **homograph** (reject). This is exactly the sense-disambiguation judgement
  a Sanskritist reader makes; e.g. distinguishing *abāla* "cocoanut" from
  *a-bāla* "not-child" requires reading the corpus sentence's meaning, not
  pattern-matching the surface form.
- **Packet C** (MW vs Whitney class conflicts, 26–32 items) — MW and
  Whitney's *Roots* **already disagree** per root; the packet asks a human
  to adjudicate which authority is right (or "both", or "other", and *which
  homonym* the conflict belongs to). Cross-checking against a third
  mechanical source (e.g. WhitneyRoots `root_class.csv`, per
  [[dcs-lemma-grammar-code-not-gana]]) does not resolve this — that source
  simply repeats Whitney's own classing, one of the two disagreeing
  authorities already shown on the sheet, so it can't break the tie.

This matches H966's own guardrail verbatim: "If the blank cells require a
HUMAN adjudication/vote (i.e. filling them means casting the reviewer's
decision, not executing an upstream-decided rule), STOP and report." No
