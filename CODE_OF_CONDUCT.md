# Code of Conduct

This project adheres to the [Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/). By participating you agree to uphold a respectful and inclusive environment for all contributors, regardless of nationality, language background, expertise level, or affiliation.

## Standards

Expected:
- Use welcoming, inclusive language. Sanskrit lexicography is a multilingual, multicultural field — assume good faith across linguistic and disciplinary backgrounds.
- Respect divergent scholarly opinions on textual readings, transliteration choices, and editorial decisions. Disagreements over a headword's classification are common and productive; disagreements should never become personal.
- Acknowledge the contributors whose work you build upon, including the typists and editors of the printed sources.

Not acceptable:
- Personal attacks, harassment, or dismissive comments about another contributor's expertise.
- Publication or sharing of others' private correspondence without consent.
- Submitting derogatory commit messages or issue comments.

## Enforcement

Concerns may be raised privately by opening an issue tagged `code-of-conduct` in the [`csl-observatory`](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/csl-observatory) repository or by emailing the maintainers (contact details in the project README).
