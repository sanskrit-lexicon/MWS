# Monier-Williams (MW) — front matter (Vorspann)

Faithful OCR transcription, plus a Russian translation, of the **front matter** of:

> **A Sanskrit-English Dictionary**, etymologically and philologically arranged with special reference to cognate Indo-European languages, by **Sir Monier Monier-Williams**, M.A., K.C.I.E. (Boden Professor of Sanskrit). *New Edition, greatly enlarged and improved*, with the collaboration of Professor E. Leumann and Professor C. Cappeller and other scholars. **Oxford, at the Clarendon Press, 1899.**

Source scans come from the Cologne Digital Sanskrit Lexicon (CDSL) csldoc build:
[mwpref.html](https://sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de/scans/csldev/csldoc/build/dictionaries/prefaces/mwpref.html). The dictionary data itself lives in [csl-orig `v02/mw/mw.txt`](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/csl-orig/blob/master/v02/mw/mw.txt); this folder covers only the prefatory pages, which are not part of `mw.txt`.

**Source language: English.** The 29 pages comprise the title page, the *Preface to the New Edition* (pp. v–ix) with a *Postscript* (p. x), and the *Introduction* (pp. xi–xxxii) in five sections:

1. **Section I** — Statement of the circumstances which led to the peculiar system of Sanskrit lexicography (pp. xi–xiii).
2. **Section II** — Explanation of the plan and arrangement (pp. xiv–xix).
3. **Section III** — Extent of Sanskrit literature comprehended in the present edition (pp. xx–xxi).
4. **Section IV** — Reasons for applying the Roman alphabet, and the method of transliteration (pp. xxii–xxx).
5. **Section V** — Acknowledgment of assistance received (pp. xxx–xxxii).

### Signatures and dates found
- **Preface** — signed *“Indian Institute, Oxford, 1899. — Monier Monier-Williams.”*
- **Postscript** (p. x) — written by his son, *M. F. Monier-Williams*, dated *May 4, 1899*; it records that the author completed the work a few days before his death at Cannes, in the south of France, on **April 11, 1899**.
- **Introduction** — ends *“Monier Monier-Williams. Indian Institute, Oxford.”* (no date printed).

The tiny digitizer running-header and footer stamps added to every CDSL scan are **omitted** from the transcription (they are not part of the original).

## Transcription notes
- English transcribed in the original 19th-century orthography (e.g. *Sanskṛit*, *connexion*, *Hindū*, *ṛi/ṛī* for ऋ/ॠ). Sanskrit/Devanāgarī kept verbatim with full diacritics.
- The comparative-alphabet table on p. xxvii (Phœnician → Greek/Roman/English and Brāhma → Nāgarī) is preserved as a Markdown table; archaic Phœnician/Brāhma glyphs that have no Unicode equivalent are marked `[Phœnician sign]` / `[Brāhma sign]` / `[sign]`, while the modern Nāgarī column is given in real Devanāgarī.
- Russian translation: personal and place names in Cyrillic (no redundant Latin in parentheses); bibliographic work-titles and all Sanskrit kept in their original script.
